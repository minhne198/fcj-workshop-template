### [Event 1](4.1-Event1/) | Automated Prompt Engineering: Enhancing LLM Output Quality

| Attribute | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Time** | 09:00, 09/05/2026 |
| **Location** | 26th Floor, Bitexco Tower, 02 Hai Trieu Street, Ben Nghe Ward, District 1, Ho Chi Minh City |
| **Role** | Attendee |

#### Brief description of main content and activities

The workshop focused on the art of designing and optimizing prompts to solve real problems such as token waste, inconsistent output, and reduced productivity when working with LLMs.

The speaker analyzed:

* **7 core components of a high-quality prompt:** Role, Instruction, Context, Input Data, Output Format, Examples, and Constraints.
* **Token Economics:** Methods for optimizing the operating cost of AI systems.
* **Advanced prompting techniques:** Chain-of-Thought (CoT), Self-Consistency, Tree-of-Thoughts (ToT), and RAG.
* **Hands-on practice:** Introduction to the Solution Architecture of Proptimizer running on AWS, using Amazon Bedrock, AWS Lambda, and Amazon DynamoDB.

#### Outcomes and value gained

> **Technology mindset:** I learned principles for communicating effectively with AI, such as being specific, focusing on what the AI should do, and using delimiters to separate context and instructions.
>
> **Practical application:** I learned how to optimize prompts for writing test cases, analyzing business documents and requirements, and applying Chain-of-Thought reasoning to complex Java and Flutter programming problems.

### [Event 2](4.2-Event2/) | AWS Vietnam Community Day 2026

| Attribute | Details |
| :--- | :--- |

| **Location** | 26th Floor, Bitexco Tower, 02 Hai Trieu Street, Ben Nghe Ward, District 1, Ho Chi Minh City |

#### Brief description of main content and activities

This large-scale technology event brought together industry experts to share the latest trends in Cloud Computing and Artificial Intelligence. Key knowledge areas included:

* **Build Second Brain:** A method for managing and organizing personal knowledge context in the AI era.
* **Amazon CloudFront:** The role of CDN in reducing latency, accelerating content delivery, improving security with AWS Shield/WAF, and optimizing cost for distributed systems.
* **Amazon Q & Multi-Agent AI:** Intelligent enterprise assistants and enterprise-level collaboration between multiple AI agents.
* **How LLMs operate:** Analysis of non-determinism through Temperature, Top-K, and Top-P parameters to control hallucination.
* **Real-world case study:** Team VIB's 36-hour LotusHacks journey to turn UTMorpho from an idea into a working product.

#### Outcomes and value gained

> **System thinking:** I gained a clearer understanding of how large enterprises apply AWS services and AI models in real operations, along with deeper knowledge of cloud infrastructure and token generation in LLMs.
>
> **Soft skills and direction:** I learned product thinking, problem-solving skills, and teamwork under high pressure. These lessons gave me a stronger foundation for improving my labs and shaping my future career path.
