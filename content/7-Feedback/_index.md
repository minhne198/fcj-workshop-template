Looking back at my journey with the First Cloud Journey (FCJ) program, I would like to express my deepest gratitude to the organizing committee and all the mentors. Below are my honest reflections and constructive feedback gathered from this internship experience:

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment & Culture**  
I was truly impressed by the friendly and highly tech-driven environment at FCJ. The gap between interns and senior experts seemed to blur thanks to everyone's openness and enthusiasm. The online support system was highly intuitive, making it easy to discuss ideas when I got stuck on AWS networking configurations or debugging code. If possible, I hope FCJ can organize more casual weekend tech-coffee sessions to foster stronger team bonding outside of work.

**2. Support from Mentors and Admins**  
The mentors are not only technically proficient but also possess excellent pedagogical skills. Instead of spoon-feeding answers, they constantly challenged me with critical questions, forcing me to read AWS documentation, debug independently, and formulate solutions for my Hotel Booking System capstone project. The robust support from the Admin team regarding procedures and practice account provisioning was also a massive plus.

**3. Practical Value to My Major**  
With a career orientation toward becoming a Cloud/DevOps Engineer, what I gained at FCJ far exceeded my initial expectations. Building a highly available web system project not only solidified my programming knowledge but also provided hands-on experience with core AWS services (VPC, EC2, RDS, ASG...). This served as a tremendous catalyst for my system design thinking.

**4. Skills Acquired**  
The program was a comprehensive bootcamp. Besides "hard skills" in cloud computing and software development lifecycles, I pocketed numerous invaluable "soft skills." These include writing clear technical documentation, confidently presenting ideas to a crowd, and managing project timelines to avoid missing deadlines.

**5. Team Spirit**  
The pressure from the final capstone project was significant, but it was amidst these challenges that I truly felt the "teamwork makes the dream work" spirit. Interns were always ready to share debugging tips and stay up late testing systems together. There was no toxic competition; everyone aimed for collective progress.

**6. Internship Support Policies**  
Being granted a sandbox account to comfortably practice on AWS without worrying about costs was a fantastic privilege. Furthermore, the flexible time management allowed me to proactively balance my time at the company without affecting the completion of my university coursework.

---

### Survey Responses

* **What was the most impressive and satisfying part?**  
What I appreciated most was being directly involved in real-world operational phases and being inspired by senior professionals. In particular, attending major community tech events organized by AWS Vietnam broadened my horizons and helped me clearly define my career roadmap.
* **Suggestions for improving the experience for future interns?**  
Because the technical content is quite "heavy," I suggest the organizing committee create a few short video tutorials or a basic "101" troubleshooting guide for common errors with core services. This would help newcomers feel less overwhelmed during their first 1-2 weeks.
* **Willingness to recommend to friends?**  
I am 100% certain I will encourage my classmates to join the next season of FCJ. This is not just a place to get an internship stamp; it is a true "training ground" that equips IT students with the practical skills needed to confidently enter the job market.

---

### Final Messages and Aspirations

* **Suggestions for enhancement:** I hope the program will incorporate more real-world case studies from large enterprises in Vietnam so we can gain a multidimensional perspective on how Cloud computing is solving actual business problems.
* **Future plans:** I deeply desire to continue staying connected with the AWS Vietnam community. If the opportunity arises, I am ready to participate as a Teaching Assistant (TA) or support the organizing committee with the next batch of interns.
* **Words of gratitude:** Once again, I sincerely thank all the Mentors, Admins, and colleagues at First Cloud Journey. I wish our program continuous, robust growth and success in its mission to nurture Vietnamese tech talent!
